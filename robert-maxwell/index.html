Every human voice has thrilling potential waiting to be discovered and unleashed. This is the voice we were born with – our natural speaking voice. But over time unhelpful habits can get in the way of our full realization of this potential.

Through a series of tailored sessions with a qualified and internationally experienced voice and acting coach you can rediscover your true vocal power – whether you’re in the theatre, the classroom, the spin studio, the boardroom, or at home with the kids.

You’ll learn simple yet effective techniques to help you free, strengthen and enrich your voice, as you enhance your innate ability to communicate with ease.

No matter what your line of work – whether you’re an actor, teacher or business professional – voice coaching can help you speak with presence and confidence. In the past I’ve worked with clients on the following:

  • Roles and auditions
  • Dialect acquisition
  • Script analysis
  • Presentations
  • Meetings and pitches
  • Workplace communication
  • Speeches and lectures
  • Interviews and press conferences
  • Vocal health and fitness

You can join a group workshop, take part in one-on-one sessions, or choose a combination of both.

One-on-one sessions allow for an in-depth investigation into your voice and body use, and are tailored to individual needs.

Group workshops are designed to suit the needs of a specific troupe of individuals, or a mix of like-minded people keen on developing their voices.

All sessions are relaxed, fun, informative, and ego-free.

Depending on your needs, your session may focus on:

  • Vocal presence, confidence and clarity
  • Vocal nuance and dexterity
  • Vocal resonance, articulation, range, pitch & placement
  • Vocal health and wellbeing
  • Freeing the breath
  • Body energies and alignment
  • Targeting nerves and anxiety
  • Techniques to reduce the risk of dysphonia and other voice conditions
  • Safe voice use for large spaces, including: room acoustics, competing with environmental noise, and other factors that may act as barriers to a healthy voice
  • Microphone technique and how not to panic if your amplification fails
  • Acting techniques that enhance vocal freedom and individual expression

All group workshops are held in-house, or at a location agreed on between me and you

I'm Rob, an experienced voice and acting coach based in Sydney, Australia. I work with performers and non-performers alike, applying principles of actor training to help people rediscover their innate vocal freedom.

I hold a Master of Fine Arts in Voice from the National Institute of Dramatic Art (NIDA) and a Bachelor of Arts from The University of Melbourne. I'm also a graduate of the Atlantic Acting School's Professional Conversatory in New York City, and have worked extensively as an actor and teacher both here in Australia and overseas.

My experience includes residencies at some of the world's top acting conversatories, such as NIDA, Yale School of Drama and Carneige Mellon School of Drama. When I'm not coaching privately, I teach voice on the Bachelor of Fine Arts (Acting) and Master of Fine Arts (Voice) courses at NIDA.

As one of Australia's few Lessac Kinesensics practitioners, I apply proven tools to guide you towards individual expression. Using the Lessac approach and an array of other vocall methodologies, I can help you develop a psycho-viseral connection to text and language, which will ultimately enhance your ability to communicate more effectively.

My teaching practise is steeped in the philosophies of Arthur Lessac, Cicely Berry, Patsy Rodenburg, Catherine Fitzmaurice, Barbara Houseman, Kristin Linklater and F.M. Alexander.
